**Project Report – Digital Literacy Project Overview**

This project covers five tasks designed to build practical digital literacy skills — not just theoretical knowledge, but actual tools and habits that are useful right now as a student.

**Task 1 – Digital Literacy Infographic** The goal was to create a one-page visual resource explaining digital literacy for batchmates. Canva was used to design the infographic, which covers what digital literacy means, safe internet practices, and professional online presence. The initiative here was to keep the design simple enough that a first-year student could read it in under two minutes and still walk away with something useful.

**Task 2 – Student Digital Portfolio** This task involved setting up a foundational professional presence online. Profiles were created on GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle. A GitHub profile README was written with basic details — branch, year, and a line about learning goals. LinkedIn's Education section was filled in with current degree and institution details. The idea was to treat these not as checkboxes but as profiles that would actually hold up if an employer or coordinator looked them up.

**Task 3 – Coding & Collaboration Platforms** HackerRank was used to complete a beginner-level coding challenge, building familiarity with how competitive coding platforms work. Alongside that, a five-question Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz was built using Google Forms — with both multiple choice and short answer questions — making it something that could realistically be shared with batchmates as part of the Digital Ambassador role.

**Task 4 – Professional Email & Etiquette Guide** Two professional emails were drafted: one requesting a deadline extension from a professor, and one expressing interest in a summer internship to a company coordinator. Both follow proper structure — clear subject line, formal greeting, concise body, and a professional sign-off. A Social Media Do's and Don'ts checklist was also created covering responsible online behaviour for college students.

**Task 5 – Cybercrime Awareness Case Study** UPI/Online Payment Fraud was chosen as the cybercrime focus, given how directly it affects college students in India. A 200–250 word case study was written covering how the fraud typically unfolds, who gets targeted, and what the consequences look like. A 'Stay Safe Online' prevention checklist followed, with eight specific tips — including two focused on UPI and financial safety — and references to the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) and Helpline 1930\. The reflection piece, written as part of the report, covered what the research revealed about SMS-based two-factor authentication and the decision to finally move to a proper password manager.

**Module 1 – Digital Literacy Foundations** This module introduced the concept of digital literacy — what it means, why it matters, and how it applies to students in an increasingly digital world. The focus was on understanding core topics such as safe internet practices, useful digital tools, professional online presence, and email etiquette, and translating that understanding into a visual format that peers could actually use.

**Module 2 – Professional Digital Presence** This module addressed the importance of building a credible online identity before entering the professional world. It covered how platforms like GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle serve different but complementary purposes, and why setting them up early — even minimally — is better than waiting until there is something to show.

**Module 3 – Coding & Collaboration Tools** This module built familiarity with two categories of tools that are directly relevant to academic and professional life: coding practice platforms and cloud-based collaboration tools. It highlighted how platforms like HackerRank and Google Workspace are not just useful for assignments but form part of the day-to-day toolkit of working professionals.

**Module 4 – Digital Communication & Etiquette** This module focused on professional communication — how to write emails that are clear, appropriately toned, and correctly structured. It also examined responsible social media behaviour, recognising that digital communication habits formed in college tend to carry forward into professional environments.

**Module 5 – Cybersecurity & Online Safety** This module covered the growing threat of cybercrime in India, with a specific focus on UPI and online payment fraud. It moved beyond general awareness into practical prevention, equipping students with specific, actionable steps they can take to protect themselves — and the knowledge of where to report incidents if something does go wrong.
