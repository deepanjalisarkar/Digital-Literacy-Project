Do’s:
1. Do think before you post — Take a moment to consider how your content might be perceived by professors, future employers, or peers. Ask yourself if you'd be comfortable with it being shared widely or seen by anyone.
2. Do protect your personal information — Avoid sharing sensitive details like your home address, phone number, class schedule, or financial information. Keep your privacy settings tight and be selective about what you make public.
3. Do use social media for genuine connections — Engage authentically with friends, join communities related to your interests or studies, and use platforms to build meaningful relationships and professional networks.
4. Do take breaks and manage your time — Set boundaries on your social media use so it doesn't interfere with studying, sleep, or mental health. Use app timers and designate phone-free times during your day.
5. Do fact-check before sharing — Verify information from credible sources before reposting or commenting. Spreading misinformation can damage your reputation and contribute to broader problems.
6. Do report harmful content — Use platform reporting tools to flag cyberbullying, harassment, or inappropriate content you encounter.
Don'ts :
1. Don't post while emotional — Avoid posting when you're angry, drunk, or upset. Wait until you've had time to cool down and think clearly about what you want to share.
2. Don't overshare personal struggles — While it's okay to be authentic, posting detailed accounts of mental health crises, family drama, or personal conflicts can have unintended consequences and may not be the best outlet for support.
3. Don't engage in arguments or flame wars — Don't waste energy arguing with strangers online or responding to inflammatory comments. Disengage from heated debates that won't lead anywhere productive.
4. Don't assume privacy on "public" accounts — Never post anything you wouldn't want a professor, employer, or parent to see. Screenshots last forever, and deleted posts can be recovered.
5. Don't compare your life to others' highlight reels — Remember that social media shows curated, filtered versions of people's lives. Don't let others' posts make you feel inadequate about your own experiences and achievements.
6. Don't ignore your digital footprint — Periodically Google yourself and review your online presence. Manage your tags, untag unflattering photos, and be aware of what's searchable about you online.
